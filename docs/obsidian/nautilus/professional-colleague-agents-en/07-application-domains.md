## 7. Application Domains

We identify nine professions or sub-professions where
Professional Colleague Agents are particularly applicable.
Order is by readiness — most ready first.

### 7.1. K-12 Education

**Practitioners**: Teachers, in all subjects and levels.
Approximately 70 million globally.

**Burden addressed**: Lesson planning, assignment generation,
grading, materials preparation, administrative documentation.

**Existing implementation**: «Обучай» (Russia), TeachAI tools
(global), Curipod (Norway/Europe). Multiple competing services
emerging.

**Maturity**: Active deployment. Tens of thousands to
millions of users globally.

**Critical mass for new entry**: High. Established players
exist. New entrants need clear differentiation.

### 7.2. Legal Practice (Sub-Specialties)

**Practitioners**: Lawyers, paralegals, legal advisors. By
sub-specialty: family law, criminal defense, immigration,
disability rights, social welfare.

**Burden addressed**: Document drafting (briefs, motions,
contracts), case research, statute lookup, deadline tracking.

**Existing implementation**: Harvey AI, CaseText, Lexis+ AI
(commercial, generalist). Limited specialized services.

**Maturity**: Beginning. Generalist tools exist; specialized
profession-specific colleagues nascent.

**Notable opportunity**: German social law / disability rights
(SGB IX, XII). Underserved by current commercial offerings.
Author's domain. (See Section 8.)

### 7.3. Medical Practice (Specific Specialties)

**Practitioners**: Physicians, particularly in primary care
and specific specialties (radiology, pathology, dermatology).

**Burden addressed**: Documentation, clinical note synthesis,
literature search, differential diagnosis support.

**Existing implementation**: Multiple commercial services
(Nuance DAX, Abridge, Suki, etc.). Hospital-integrated
deployments common.

**Maturity**: Rapid deployment in 2024-2026. Significant
healthcare provider adoption.

**Critical mass for new entry**: High. Major commercial
players. Specialized opportunities in underserved
sub-specialties.

### 7.4. Social Work and Case Management

**Practitioners**: Social workers, case managers, community
organizers, particularly in welfare systems.

**Burden addressed**: Client documentation, resource matching,
deadline tracking, regulatory compliance, communication
drafting.

**Existing implementation**: Limited. Some case management
software with AI features. No comprehensive Professional
Colleague Agent deployment.

**Maturity**: Significant opportunity. Underserved profession
serving vulnerable populations.

**Foundation alignment**: Strong. Diakonie, Caritas,
equivalent organizations are natural deployment partners.

### 7.5. Mental Health Care

**Practitioners**: Therapists, counselors, clinical psychologists.

**Burden addressed**: Session note synthesis, treatment
planning support, literature review, billing documentation.

**Existing implementation**: Emerging. Note-taking tools
appearing. Concerns about AI's role in therapeutic context
significant.

**Maturity**: Early. Ethical concerns slow deployment.
Privacy and clinical accuracy critical.

**Caution**: Mental health is high-sensitivity domain. Agent
must be deeply integrated with clinical safety frameworks.

### 7.6. Architecture and Engineering

**Practitioners**: Architects, civil engineers, structural
engineers.

**Burden addressed**: Code compliance checking, drawing
generation, calculation routines, regulatory navigation,
specification writing.

**Existing implementation**: Specialized software with AI
features. AutoCAD, Revit, others adding AI. Limited true
Professional Colleague Agents.

**Maturity**: Emerging. Significant opportunity for
profession-specific deployment.

### 7.7. Accounting and Tax Practice

**Practitioners**: Accountants, tax preparers, auditors.

**Burden addressed**: Document review, regulatory compliance,
calculation routines, audit preparation.

**Existing implementation**: Multiple commercial services
(Karbon, TaxAct AI, others). Active competitive market.

**Maturity**: Active. Profession evolving rapidly with AI
deployment.

### 7.8. Government Service Delivery

**Practitioners**: Civil servants in benefit determination,
licensing, immigration processing.

**Burden addressed**: Application review, eligibility
determination support, documentation, citizen communication
drafting.

**Existing implementation**: Limited and politically
sensitive. Government deployment of AI in citizen-facing
services raises significant concerns.

**Maturity**: Slow and contested. Important but careful
deployment required.

**Caveat**: This domain is particularly susceptible to
several risks — mediation collapse, bias, profession capture.
Especially careful design needed.

### 7.9. Academic Research Support

**Practitioners**: Researchers across disciplines.

**Burden addressed**: Literature review, draft writing,
methodology selection support, citation management, grant
application drafting.

**Existing implementation**: Multiple emerging services
(Elicit, Consensus, Scite). Active development.

**Maturity**: Active deployment. Researcher attitudes mixed.
