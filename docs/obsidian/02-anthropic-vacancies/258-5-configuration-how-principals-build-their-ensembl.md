## 5. Configuration: How Principals Build Their Ensembles

A central question for Composite Skills Agents: how does a 
principal arrive at their specific configuration?

### 5.1. The Configuration Problem

A principal entering an ecosystem with hundreds of available 
sub-agents faces a configuration problem. They do not yet know:
- Which specializations are most relevant to their work
- Which combinations work well together
- Which sub-agents are highest quality
- How their configuration should evolve over time

This is similar to how a new yoga student facing hundreds of 
possible teachers doesn't know which twenty to pursue. The 
configuration emerges from a process, not a single decision.

### 5.2. Three Configuration Approaches

We identify three approaches with different strengths and 
trade-offs.

**Approach 1 — Self-directed.** The principal browses the 
registry, selects sub-agents based on their self-assessment, 
and configures their composite agent.

Strengths: Full agency. Principal develops deep knowledge of 
the registry. No external influence on selection.

Weaknesses: Requires substantial self-knowledge. New 
practitioners often don't know what they need. May miss 
valuable specializations they hadn't considered.

**Approach 2 — AI-assisted recommendation.** The composite 
agent (or a separate recommendation system) analyzes the 
principal's stated goals, work history, and preferences, then 
suggests an initial configuration. Principal modifies as 
needed.

Strengths: Lower barrier to entry. Surfaces specializations 
the principal might not have considered. Faster initial setup.

Weaknesses: Risk of recommendation bias. Algorithmic suggestions 
may converge toward popular configurations, reducing diversity. 
Principal may accept recommendations passively without learning 
the registry.

**Approach 3 — Mentor-guided.** An experienced practitioner — 
ideally one who has developed their own composite practice — 
helps the principal think through their configuration.

Strengths: Deep human judgment about what configurations work. 
Mentor knows what the principal's stated goals actually require. 
Builds relationship beyond the technology.

Weaknesses: Requires access to a mentor. Mentor's perspective 
may bias selection. Doesn't scale automatically.

### 5.3. Hybrid Configuration

In practice, the best configuration process combines all three 
approaches:

1. **Initial AI recommendation** based on principal's stated 
   profile generates a starting configuration
2. **Self-directed exploration** lets principal browse, adjust, 
   add specializations they recognize as relevant
3. **Mentor review** (optional) provides experienced human 
   perspective, especially for principals new to their field

This three-step process echoes how human professional development 
actually works: training programs provide initial frameworks, 
self-directed learning fills gaps, and mentors provide deep 
guidance.

### 5.4. Configuration Evolution

A principal's configuration is not static. Specializations 
change over time:
- New specializations enter their work as careers evolve
- Old specializations become less relevant as priorities shift
- New sub-agents become available in the registry
- Old sub-agents may be deprecated

The composite agent should help the principal monitor 
configuration health:
- Which sub-agents are most used?
- Which are rarely consulted?
- Are there new sub-agents that match the principal's recent 
  work patterns?

Periodic configuration review (perhaps annually) keeps the 
composite agent aligned with the principal's evolving practice.

### 5.5. Templates and Starting Points

For many professions, common configurations exist. A new social 
law lawyer might benefit from a starting template:
- General SGB IX sub-agent
- General SGB XII sub-agent  
- Procedural law sub-agent
- BSG case law sub-agent
- General drafting sub-agent

This template provides a reasonable starting configuration. The 
practitioner then specializes by adding sub-agents specific to 
their developing practice (e.g., disability rights specifically, 
or social welfare for elderly clients, or working with mental 
health conditions).

Templates are starting points, not endpoints. Every practitioner 
modifies their configuration over time.
