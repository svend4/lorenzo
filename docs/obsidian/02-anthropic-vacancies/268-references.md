## References

### Foundational Sources for Composite Architecture

- Anderson, J. R. (1983). *The Architecture of Cognition*. 
  Harvard University Press. (On modular cognitive architecture.)
- Minsky, M. (1986). *The Society of Mind*. Simon & Schuster. 
  (On cognition as ensemble of specialized agents.)
- Csikszentmihalyi, M. (1997). *Creativity: Flow and the 
  Psychology of Discovery and Invention*. Harper. (On how 
  expertise develops through specialization combined with 
  synthesis.)

### Pattern of Composite Mastery

- Sennett, R. (2008). *The Craftsman*. Yale University Press. 
  (On how skilled practice combines learned specializations.)
- Dreyfus, H. L., & Dreyfus, S. E. (1986). *Mind Over Machine: 
  The Power of Human Intuition and Expertise in the Era of the 
  Computer*. Free Press. (On stages of skill acquisition.)

### Multi-Agent Coordination

- Wooldridge, M. (2009). *An Introduction to MultiAgent Systems*. 
  Wiley. (Foundational text.)
- Gilbert, N., & Troitzsch, K. G. (2005). *Simulation for the 
  Social Scientist*. Open University Press. (On modeling 
  ensembles.)

### Practical Examples

- Stanier, J. (2020). *Become an Effective Software Engineering 
  Manager*. Pragmatic Bookshelf. (On how engineers combine 
  specialized knowledge.)
- Susskind, R., & Susskind, D. (2015). *The Future of the 
  Professions*. Oxford University Press. (On how professions 
  are evolving in the AI era.)
